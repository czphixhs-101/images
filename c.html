Section 01

What is Artificial Intelligence?

AI is the simulation of human intelligence in machines — enabling them to think, learn, and make decisions.

Concept
AI mimics human cognition at scale

Narrow AI (ANI)

Designed for specific tasks — Siri, recommendations, spam filters. This is what we use today.

General AI (AGI)

Hypothetical AI with human-level intelligence across all domains. Still theoretical.

Super AI (ASI)

Surpasses human intelligence in every way. Science fiction for now.

Key Insight: All tools we'll discuss (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.) are Narrow AI — incredibly powerful at specific tasks but not conscious.

Section 01

How LLMs Actually Work

Large Language Models predict the next word — but the pipeline is fascinating.

Tokenization

Text broken into tokens (words/subwords)

Helloworldhowareyou?

Embedding

Tokens → numbers (vectors) that capture meaning

Attention Mechanism

Weighs which words relate to each other — understands context

Next Token Prediction

Predicts most likely next token from training patterns

Output Generation

Repeats to generate complete, coherent responses

Architecture
Transformer Neural Network layers

Fun Facts

  • • GPT-4 trained on ~13 trillion tokens
  • • Training cost: estimated $100M+
  • • "Transformer" invented by Google in 2017
  • • LLMs don't "understand" — they predict patterns

Prompt Engineering

Prompt Engineering

The skill of crafting instructions that get the best possible output from AI. This is your superpower.

❌ Bad Prompt

"Write about marketing"

Too vague — generic, unhelpful output

✓ Good Prompt

"Write a 500-word LinkedIn post about content marketing trends in 2025. Professional tone, 3 bullet points, end with a question."

Specific — length, platform, tone, structure

Skill
Prompt Engineering = Talking to AI effectively
Prompt Engineering

The CREATE Framework

C

Context

Background info. "I'm a startup founder in fintech..."

R

Role

Who should AI be? "Act as a senior strategist..."

E

Exact Task

What specifically to do. "Write a 300-word intro..."

A

Audience

Who is this for? "For CTOs at enterprise companies..."

T

Tone & Format

Style. "Conversational, use H2 headers, bullet points"

E

Examples

Show what you want. "Here's a sample of the style..."

Complete Example

[Context] I run a SaaS company with 50 employees. [Role] Act as an HR expert. [Exact Task] Write a company-wide email announcing hybrid work policy. [Audience] All employees aged 25-55. [Tone] Warm but professional. [Examples] Like this previous email: "Team, we're excited to share..." — [Format] Email with subject line, greeting, 3 key points, closing.

Prompt Engineering

Advanced Prompt Techniques

Chain-of-Thought

Force AI to think step by step

Example

"Solve this problem. Think step by step and explain your reasoning at each stage before the final answer."

Few-Shot Learning

Give examples of input→output first

Example

"Classify: 'Love it!' → Positive. 'Terrible.' → Negative. 'It's okay.' → Neutral. Now: 'Pretty good but could improve' →"

Role-Playing

Assign a specific persona

Example

"You are a senior UX researcher at Google. Review this wireframe for information hierarchy and accessibility."

Constraint Setting

Set boundaries for quality control

Example

"Write a product description. Max 100 words, no adjectives, exactly 3 features, simple language a 12-year-old understands."

Section 03 — Content

Blog Posts & SEO Content

AI can write full blog posts with proper structure, keywords, and SEO optimization.

Step-by-Step Process

Generate Topic Ideas

"Give me 10 blog topics about [niche] with high search volume"

Create Outline

"Create a detailed blog outline with H2, H3 sections"

Write Each Section

Don't write the whole article at once — go section by section

Add SEO Elements

Meta title, description, internal linking suggestions

Human Review & Edit

Always add your voice, experience, and fact-check

Blog Prompt Template

"Write a 1500-word blog post about [TOPIC]. Target keyword: [KEYWORD]. Include: compelling H1, 4-5 H2 sections, bullet points, conclusion with CTA. Tone: [friendly/authoritative]. Audience: [describe reader]. Use keyword naturally 3-5 times."

Pro Tips

  • •Write section by section for better quality
  • •Ask AI to add personal anecdotes or data points
  • •Use "make it more conversational" to refine
  • •Never publish AI content without human review

Video Prompt Formula: [Subject] + [Action/Movement] + [Camera angle/movement] + [Lighting] + [Style] + [Duration] + [Aspect Ratio]

Do ✓
  • • Describe camera movements
  • • Specify lighting conditions
  • • Keep prompts under 100 words
  • • Mention duration explicitly
Don't ✗
  • • Request complex dialogue
  • • Expect perfect text rendering
  • • Use vague descriptions
  • • Request copyrighted characters
